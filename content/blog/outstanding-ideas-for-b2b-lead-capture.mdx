### Outstanding Idea #2: The "Intent Score" (Filtering Out the Noise)

One of the biggest risks with "Deanonymization" is noise. You don't want your sales team calling a university student researching a paper, or a competitor spying on your pricing.

**The Fix:** **Behavioral Scoring.**

This is where "Outstanding Ideas" separate from "Spam." You should only reach out to companies showing **High Intent**. Happierleads allows you to set granular filters:

- **Page Depth:** Only flag companies that visit >3 pages.

- **Critical Pages:** Only flag companies that visit `/pricing` or `/case-studies`.

- **Time on Site:** Ignore anyone who bounces in under 30 seconds.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is identifying anonymous visitors legal (GDPR/CCPA)?

Yes, it is legal. Happierleads identifies Companies, not individuals. It tells you "Coca-Cola visited your site," which is public corporate data. It does not tell you "John Smith visited from his iPhone." When you "Reveal Contacts," you are accessing a B2B database of business emails, which is standard practice in B2B sales. Always ensure your Privacy Policy mentions that you use tracking technology.

How accurate is the data?

No tool is 100%. If everyone at a company is working from home (remote), the match rate drops because they are using residential IPs. However, Happierleads uses a proprietary database to map remote workers back to corporate networks where possible. Expect a match rate of 30-60% of your total traffic.

Can I use this for a small blog?

You can, but it works best if you sell B2B services or high-ticket products. If you sell $10 t-shirts, identifying the company won't help you much. If you sell $2,000 consulting packages, identifying one lead pays for the software for 3 years.
